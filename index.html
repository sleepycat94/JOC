Book Review

The Handmaid's Tale by Margaret Atwood, 1985

The Handmaid's Tale Book Review

The dystopian novel, The Handmaid's Tale, takes place in a futuristic, patriarchal society known as the Republic of Gilead. It takes place in what we know as the modern-day US. The book asks us a question and warns us that if we leave patriarchal society unchecked, it will develop into a totalitarian, oppressive regime. Atwood illustrates that there aren't that many differences between dystopias and utopias, and that we are closer than ever before to removing free will and thought from our existence. Women are targeted by the regime and made out to be slaves and to think and act like second-class citizens in the novel.

Movie Review

Poor Things, December 8th, 2023

Poor Things Movie Review (click for Trailer)

Poor Things is an alternative movie that takes place at an unspecified point in time. The main character and protagonist, Bella Baxter, is a woman who committed suicide only to be brought back to life by an eccentric scientist (with a baby's brain). Her development as a human being is shown throughout the novel, and she discovers what there is to like (and hate) about the epitome(s) of human existence. She is a strong-willed woman who is repeatedly taken advantage of by a canniving and sly suitor who is hellbent on manipulating her for his own pleasure. She discovers more about herself throughout the movie's runtime.
